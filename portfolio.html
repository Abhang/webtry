Global Oil Supply Crisis Response

Ensuring business continuity during the Ukraine war by redesigning global oil supply strategies.

Role: Supply Chain Strategy & Consulting

The Challenge

The outbreak of the Ukraine war trapped critical sunflower oil reserves. This threatened to halt production lines across multiple global markets, risking substantial revenue loss and eroding consumer trust.

The Solution

I pivoted the project from network design to crisis management. Analyzing reserves across APAC and EMEA, I engineered a temporary "multi-oil blend" strategy optimized for cost, availability, and regional regulatory compliance.

Quantified Results

  • 90% Business Continuity ensured for affected markets despite the blockade.
  • Zero Production Halts achieved by rapidly deploying alternative oil blends across diverse regulatory landscapes.
  • Strategic Resilience established by diversifying sourcing away from single-point-of-failure regions.

$10.6B Global Supply Network Optimization

Redesigning the global supply network for a major fast-food giant to ensure resilience and cost efficiency.

Role: Project Lead & Lead Consultant

The Challenge

The client's $10.6B global potato supply network (7.4M tonnes) faced 40% escalating costs and critical vulnerabilities. With supply buffers below 2%, any climate or geopolitical disruption threatened global operations.

The Solution

I built a Mixed Integer Linear Programming (MILP) model with over 30,000 constraints. It optimized for near-shoring, climate risk, and market-specific needs (Halal/Kosher), implementing a "just-in-case" strategy for critical market backup.

Quantified Results

  • $480 Million Saved in projected costs through network redistribution and logistics optimization.
  • 50% Increase in Supply Lanes enhancing resilience against single-point failures in the global node network.
  • Global Compliance integrated strictly for Kosher (Israel) and Halal (Middle East) markets within the mathematical model.
